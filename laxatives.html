Laxatives Drugs

Define Constipation
✓ Constipation is an acute or chronic condition that is characterized by the difficulties in passage of stool
What are the key notes on Constipation
✓ Hard Stool
✓ Pain and straining on passing stool
✓ Final passage of small stool
What are some of the the factors that can make a person susceptible to Constipation

✓ Certain medications
✓ Older age group
✓ Low fibre diet
✓ Mental health conditions e.g. depression or an eating disorder
✓ Being a woman
✓ Being dehydrated
✓ COLMB is the mnemonic u can use
Mention some drugs, which when taken may cause constipation
✓ Antihistamines & Alluminium containing Drugs
✓ Bile Acid Sequestrants & Benzodiazepines
✓ Calcium channel blocker & Calcium Supplements/ antacids
✓ Diuretics
✓ there are many more and the mnemonic to use for all of them is ABCD-STOP
What are the major complications of Constipation
✓ Anal fissure - tearing of anal skin
✓ Hemorrhoids - veins of the anus swelling
✓ Anal impaction - Stool failng to be expelled
✓ Rectal prolapse - intestine that protrudes from the anus
What are some of the lifestyle modifications that can work n treating constipation
✓ Increases of dietry fibre ingestion
✓ Adequate fluid intake
✓ Regular exercise
When must you resort to a laxative when treating constipation
✓ If dietary modification fails to achieve the control of constipation
How do laxatives work
✓ Laxatives are a group of drugs that help stimulate intestinal motility and increase the movement of material through the bowel. Thiis decreases the intestinal transit time hence facilitating defecation
What are the indications for laxative
✓ a constipation state where dietry modification has proven insuffecient
✓ To evacuate the bowel during surgery or any diagnostic examination
✓ In the elimination of drugs or poisons from the intestnal tract in case of drug overdose or poisoning
list the types of Laxatives
✓ Bulk forming Laxatives
✓ Osmotic Laxatives
✓ Surfactant Laxatives (Stool softeners)
✓ Stimulant Laxatives
✓ The mnemonic is BOSS
What are bulk forming laxatives
✓ These are indigestible hydrophilic substances which resemble natural dietary fibre
List examples of laxatives
✓ Calcium polycarbophyl
✓ Methyl cellulose
✓ Psyllium hydrophyllic mucilloid
Explain the mechanism of action of bulk forming laxatives
✓ They absorb and retain water in the intestinal lumen thereby, increasing the mass of intestinal content
✓ This causes eventual distention of the intestinal wall and thus leading to stimulation of peristalsis
Why is it important for the patient to take bulk-forming laxatives with a full cup of water?
✓ To help enhance adequate hydration and prevent intestinal obstruction
By giving a reason for your answer name the most common condition that the bulk forming laxatives are used to treat
✓ chronic constipation
✓ this is coz they are the safest and most physiologic form of laxatives and rarely cause adverse effects
Psylium hydrophyllic mucilloid is also used in treating Diarrhoea, What property does it have that enables it to perform this function
✓ The bulk forming laxatives have the ability to absorb water and irritant substances such as bile salts, hence making them suitable for the treatment of Diarrhoea
Another group of drugs used in the treatment of constipation are Osmotic drugs. Name the drugs classification that falls under this group
✓ Poorly Absorbed Sugars
» Lactulose
» Polyethylene glycol (PEG)
✓ Poorly absorbed salts (saline laxatives)

What are different ways in which These Osmotic laxatives can be administered to a patient
✓ orally
✓ enemas (an injection of a liquid through the anus to stimulate evacuation; sometimes used for diagnostic purposes)
Which of the two groups of Osmotic Laxatives when given at sufficient dosses will act rapidly to stimulate defecation
✓ Saline Laxatives
Elaborate the mechanism of action of Osmotic Laxatives
✓ These substances attract and retain water in the intestinal lumen leading to increase intraluminal pressure. This eventually stimulate peristalsis
Name two indications for Sodium phosphate
✓ Bowel evacuation in patients scheduled for surgery/diagnostic exams
✓ Patients with drug overdose or poisoning
What is the Major indication Of magnesium Oxide as a Laxatives
✓ can be used in lower doses to prevent constipation like in patients receiving opioids
What group of osmotic laxatives is used to treat Chronic conditions ?
✓ Poorly Absorbed Sugars
What group of osmotic laxatives is used to treat Acute conditions ? give reasons for your answer
✓ Saline Laxatives
✓ Excessive use of saline laxatives can lead to loss of fluids and electrolytes
✓ They act rapidly when given at sufficient doses hence them being more suitable for acute conditions
What sort of patients must caution be taken before administering osmotic laxatives to them, and why ?
✓ Patients with renal impairment
✓ They may not be able to properly excrete saline laxatives that are absorbed into circulation

Describe the mechanism of action of the stimulant laxatives
✓ These agents are stimulative in nature where they act directly on the intestinal mucosa to alter fluid secretion and subsequent stimulation of peristalsis
