Ranitidine

Class:

Histamine 2 Receptor Antagonists (H2As)

Mechanism: PIG

  • Blocks Histamine 2 receptors, leading to:
    • Pepsinogen activation failure to pepsin
    • Intrinsic Factor low secretion but not enough to reduce vitamin B12 absorption
    • Gastric Acid secretion prevention

Pharmacokinetics:

  • Absorption: GUT. then undergoes hepatic inactivation b4 secretion thru urine
  • has long duration of action inspite of having a half-life of 2-3hrs
  • given once or twice a day

Indications: PH GAD

  • PUD & Heartburn
  • GERD/GORD & Allergic reactions when used in combination with H1 receptor blockers & Dyspepsia
  • A dose is recommended to supress Acid secretion the whole night

Side Effects: ABCD HART

  • AV Block, Bradycardia, Confusion, Diarrhoea & Depression & Dizziness
  • Headache & Hypersensitivity rxns, Altered liver function, Rash, Tiredness

Drug Interactions:

  • Due to its ability to Change gastric pH it lowers absoption of Drugs like iron & Atazanavir while increasing absoprtion of drugs like saquinavir

Calcium polycarbophyl

Class:

Bulk forming laxatives

Mechanism:

  • absorb and retain water in the intestinal lumen thereby, increasing the mass of intestinal content
  • This causes eventual distention of the intestinal wall and thus leading to stimulation of peristalsis

Indications:

  • Chronic Constipation
  • treatment of diarrhea as it absorbs H2O and irritants

Side Effects:

  • may cause dehydration and Intestinal obstruction
  • To avoid this, take this drug with a full cup of water
  • Rarely cause Adverse Effects

Ketoconazole

Class:

Antifungal (Azole)

Mechanism:

Inhibits fungal cell membrane synthesis by blocking the enzyme lanosterol 14-alpha-demethylase, preventing conversion of lanosterol to ergosterol.

Indications:

Fungal infections (e.g., candidiasis, dermatophytosis)

Side Effects:

  • Hepatotoxicity
  • GI disturbances
  • hormonal imbalance

Atazanavir

Class:

Antiretroviral (Protease Inhibitor)

Mechanism:

Inhibits the HIV-1 protease enzyme, preventing viral protein cleavage and maturation of infectious particles

Indications:

HIV/AIDS (as part of combination therapy)

Side Effects:

  • Hyperbilirubinemia
  • GI disturbances
  • rash
  • elevated liver enzymes
