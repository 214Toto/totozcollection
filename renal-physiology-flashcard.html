Renal System

The rate at which different substances are excreted in urine is as a result of three renal physiological processes, namely ;
✓ Glomerular filtration
✓ Tubular reabsorption
✓ Tubular secretion
What percent of water and other usefull solutes are reabsorbed back into the Blood
✓ 99%
Tubular secretion is a process that moves substances from the.......... to the ...........
✓ Blood to the filtered fluid
How do you calculate the rate of excretion of any solute
✓ Excretion of any solute = glomerular filtration + secretion - reabsorption
Define the folowing terms; (1) Glomerular filtrate (2) Filtration Fraction
✓ Glomerular filtrate ⇉ The fluid that enters the capsular space
✓ Filtration Fraction ⇉ The fraction of blood plasma in the afferent arterioles of the kidneys that becomes glomerular filtrate
The Daily volume of the Glomerular Filtrate in Males and Females is?
✓ Females ⇒ 150 liters
✓ Male ⇒ 180 liters
What structures make up the Filtration Membrane
✓ endothelial cells of glomerular capillaries
✓ Basement Membrane
✓ Layer of epithelial cells forming the inner layer of the Glomerular Capsule ( Visceral layer )
What is the role of the mesangial cells
✓ These contractile cells help in regulaton of glomerular filtration. Contraction of mesangial cells reduces the available surface area, and glomerular filtration decreases
Name some of the features of the Filtration Membrane that enable it to perform its function effectively
✓ large surface area
✓ thin and porous membrane
✓ high glomerular capillary blood pressure
✓ some of its components are fixed with (-) charges
What is the main component that each layer of the Filtration Membrane layers prevents to cross
✓ endothelial cells of glomerular capillaries - prevents blood cells but allows all Components of plasma to go through
✓ Basement Membrane - prevents filtration of larger proteins
✓ Visceral layer - prevents filtration of medium-sized proteins
Albumin is only about 6nm, whereas the pore size of the Filtration Membrane is about 10nm, yet Albumin is restricted from filtration. Why is this so ?
✓ Albumin is a negatively charged particle and Because of the negative charge that The Filtration Membrane has, there is an electrostatic repulsion exerted on Albumin inhibiting it from crossng the barrier
What is meant by Glomerular filtration rate (GFR)
✓ amount of filtrate formed in all the renal corpuscles of both kidneys each minute
✓ 125ml/min for a kama healthy person
What are the consequences if the GFR is too high or too low
✓ Too high – substances pass too quickly and are not reabsorbed
✓ Too low – nearly all reabsorbed and some waste products not adequately excreted
Define the Net Filtration Pressure and elaborate how you calculate it
✓ NFP is the total pressure that promotes filtration
✓ NFP = GBHP – CHP – BCOP
✓ NFP = 55mmHg - 15mmHg - 30mmHg
✓ NFP = 10 mmHg
In some kidney diseases, glomerular capillaries are damaged and become so permeable that plasma proteins enter glomerular filtrate. From the physiological point of view and in relation to the NFP forces, how will this change in the glomerular capillaries causes edema
✓ loss of plasma proteins into the Filtrate will cause colloid osmotic pressure in the capsule that will draw water out of the blood.
✓ NFP will ⇑ and colloid osmotic pressure in tho blood decreases..
✓ Blood volume will ⇊ as interstitial fluid increases leadin to edema
What are the 3 mechanisms that regulate the GFR
✓ Renal Autoregulation
✓ Neural regulation
✓ Hormonal regulation
Describe the renal Autoregulation
✓ This is where the kidneys themselves maintain constant renal blood flow and GFR using Two mechanisms, Myogenic Mechanism and Tubuloglomerular mechanism
Discuss what Myogenic Mechanism is
✓ occurs when stretching triggers contraction of smooth muscle cells in afferent arterioles – reduces GFR
Write chorts notes on the Tubuloglomerular Mechanism
✓ This is a mechanism where the Juxtaglomerular Apparatus provides a feedback mechansim that inhibits NO release causing afferent arterioles to constrict
✓
Explain what happens during the Neural regulation
✓ Since some of the blood vessels in the kidney are supplied by sympathetic ANS, they stimulate the release of norepinephrine causing vasconstriction
What happens durng the Neural regulation in Moderate and Greater Stimulation
✓ Moderate stimulation – both afferent and efferent arterioles constrict to same degree and GFR decreases
✓ Greater stimulation constricts afferent arterioles more and GFR drops
What of the Hormonal Regulation, what can you say about it?
✓ it involves hormones like Angiotensin II that contricts the afferent arteriole more......
✓ Atrial natriuretic peptide increases GFR – stretching of atria causes release, increases capillary surface area for filtration
Discuss Tubular reabsorption
✓ – return of most of the filtered water and many solutes to the bloodstream, where about 99% of them are reabsorbed through active and passive processes
✓ Proximal convoluted tubule cells make largest contribution
Discuss Tubular Seceretion
✓ transfer of material from blood into tubular fluid
✓ It helps control blood pH & eliminate toxic substances from the body
What are some of the reabsorption routes used....
✓ Paracellular reabsorption - sunstances are passively transported via tight junctions between adjacent tubule cells
✓ Transcellular reabsorption - occurs through the cells themselves Active or Passive Process: Unlike paracellular reabsorption, transcellular transport can involve both active and passive mechanisms, depending on the substance.
