Pharyngeal Apparatus

The pharyngeal apparatus consists of:
✓ CAMP
✓ pharyngeal clefts (grooves)
✓ pharyngeal arches
✓ pharyngeal membranes.
✓ pharyngeal pouches
To which region of the body dooes the pharyngeal apparatus contribute to in terms of development
✓ head & neck region
✓ e.g., face, neck, definitive mouth, pharynx, and larynx
When ad where do the pharyngeal arches appear in the kama growing embryo
✓ When → Early in the 4th week of intrauterine development
✓ Where → in the lateral wall & floor of primitive pharynx caudal to stomatodeum
These arches have pounches and grooves in the inner and outer aspect respectively, what are their names ?
✓ Inner aspect → pharyngeal pouches
✓ outer aspect → pharyngeal clefts
elaborate on the number, position and lining of the Pharyngeal grooves
✓ Number = 4
✓ position = external
✓ lining = ectoderm
elaborate on the number, position and lining of the Pharyngeal pouches
✓ Number = 4
✓ position = internal
✓ lining = endoderm
A typical pharyngeal arch consists of A core of mesoderm covered externally by ectoderm and internally by endoderm. Where is the mesoderm derived from ?
✓ paraxial mesoderm
✓ lateral plate mesoderm
✓ neural crest cells
Outline the components formed from the mesoderm of each Arch
✓ Striated muscle
✓ A cartilaginous bar/rod
✓ A pharyngeal arch artery
✓ A nerve
✓ C-MAN
Draw and label a pharyngeal arch and its components
✓
The arch mesoderm is derived from three components. What does the one derived from the paraxial mesoderm and the neural crest cells give rise to ?
✓ Paraxial Mesoderm gives rise to most of the muscles of head and neck region
✓ Neural crest cells gives rise to skeletal elements and connective tissue of the head and neck region
What is the fate of the cartilaginous rod of the arch
✓ forms the skeletal derivatives of the arch with part of it forming permanent skeletal elements (e.g., bone and cartilage) while the other parts of it dissappear
✓ Cartilage may disappears but its perichondrium persists to form ligament/raph
What is the connection of the Arch Arteries
✓ are connected ventrally to ventral aorta and they pass around the primitive pharynx to open in the dorsal aorta
What is the role of the nerve of the arches
✓ provides motor innervation to muscles derived from arch
✓ sensory innervation to skin and mucosa derived from the arch
muscles of the pharyngeal arch are derived from which specific portion of the arch mesoderm
The mesoderm Derived from;
✓ paraxial mesoderm
✓ lateral plate mesoderm
Write short notes on the skeletal attachment and nerve suplly of the Pharyngeal arch muscles
✓ muscles are either attached to skeletal elements developed from the same arch or migrate elsewhere
✓ However, They are supplied by nerve of the arch from which they develop
